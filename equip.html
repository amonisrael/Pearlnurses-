Nursing Equipment Reviews: Best Tools for Ugandan Wards ​A nurse is only as good as their tools. We’ve tested the most common equipment available in Kampala and online to help you spend your money wisely. ​1. The Stethoscope: The Heart of Your Kit ​In a noisy ward, a clear stethoscope is the difference between missing a murmur and saving a life. ​The Gold Standard: 3M Littmann Classic III. ​Pros: Extremely durable, high acoustic sensitivity, easy-to-clean diaphragm. ​Where to buy: Available at major medical supply stores in Wandegeya or Nakasero. ​The Budget Choice: ADC Adscope. ​Pros: Much cheaper than a Littmann but offers 80% of the quality. Great for students. ​2. Blood Pressure Cuffs (Sphygmomanometers) ​While many wards have digital machines, a manual cuff is more reliable during power outages or for patients with irregular heartbeats. ​Our Pick: Aneroid Sphygmomanometer (Manual). ​Why: Digital machines often lose calibration. A manual cuff with a large, easy-to-read dial is essential for the "community outreach" bag. ​Tip: Always check that the cuff size is appropriate for your patient (Adult vs. Pediatric). ​3. Medical Scissors & Forceps ​Don't use your teeth or blunt office scissors to open IV bags or cut bandages! ​Must-Have: Stainless Steel Lister Bandage Scissors. ​Why: The blunt tip prevents you from accidentally cutting the patient’s skin when removing dressings. ​Artery Forceps: Essential for clamping tubes or assisting in minor wound dressing. ​4. Pocket Essentials ​Penlight: Look for LED versions for checking pupil response (PEARL). ​Medical Watch: A watch with a second hand is mandatory for counting pulse and respirations. Avoid smartwatches if they require frequent charging! ​5.
